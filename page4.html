Exploring Wavelength and Frequency in GNU Radio

In this exercise, students will create a flowgraph using GNU Radio to explore the relationship between wavelength and frequency. By adjusting the frequency of a signal and observing the corresponding changes in wavelength, students gain a clearer understanding of these fundamental properties of electromagnetic waves.

Detecting the Radio Signal From a Car Remote

In this section of the lab, students will use an RTL-SDR (software-defined radio) and GNU Radio to detect the signal emitted by a car key fob. By tuning into the specific frequency range used by key fobs, students can observe the short bursts of data transmitted when the button is pressed. This hands-on exercise allows students to explore how everyday devices like car keys use electromagnetic waves to send signals, reinforcing their understanding of radio frequencies, signal modulation, and real-world applications of SDR technology.
